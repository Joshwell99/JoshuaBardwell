Intro

My name is Joshua Bardwell, 2nd year astronomy graduate student at San Diego State University. My interests in astronomy first began during my undergrad when I interned for the James Webb Space Telescope under the Northrop Grumman Executive team. Then, in my fourth year of undergrad at CalPoly San Luis Obispo I began to do research in Brightest Cluster Galaxy mergers and morphology, which soldified my passion in astronomy and astrophysics. In the summer prior to attending graduate school, I had the pleasure to intern for NASA Goddard Space Flight center where I developed DARMA, a computer program which analyzes complex spectra and determines their molecular compositions.

In addition to my love for science and computer programming, I have a strong passion for clothing and logo design! At my first internship for the JWST I spent the first half of my session designing NASA T-shirts to promote the JWST launch!

Scientific Research

Black Hole Binaries and LISA!

Advancements within the field of gravitational wave (GW) science have provided confirmations of our understandings of general relativity and new insights into exotic binary systems such as black-hole binaries! For my master's thesis, I have been working with NASA to expand upon waveform models of extreme-mass ratio inspirals (EMRIs, a stellar-mass black hole in binary with a supermassive blackhole (SMBH)) for the future LISA mission!

My initial work within this project involved generating GWs and understanding how they evolve as certain parameters change. The video above shows how the FFT of a GW changes as the mass of a SMBH is increased from 1e6 to 1e8 solar masses.

Comets and Protoplanetary Archeaology!

Comets can be thought as fossils which contain information of our early solar system when it was a protoplanetary disk. During my internship with SubLIME at NASA Goddard, I worked with spectra of experimentally created comets which have been incinerated by a ultraviolet laser!

While working for this lab, I developed a program called DARMA (Detection Automation of Rotational-peaks and Molecular Assignment). This automated-program is able to determine the molecular composition of the gaseous by-products which sublimate off these cometary nuclei!

BIG Galaxies and Cannabilization!

Brightest Cluster Galaxies (BCGs) are the largest known galaxies in the local universe. The growth and morphology of these unfathomably large objects strictly depends on the cannabilzation of smaller galaxies within its vicinty! For my undergraduate thesis, I researched the evolution of BCG's and their contribution to the Interstellar Medium.

Within this project, I analyzed datasets to better understand the contribution of differing sized companion galaxies towards the growth of BCGs. I then documented my findings into a paper, which is out for review!

Creative Works

DARMA, Detection Automation of Rotational peaks and Molecular Assignment, is a pythonic program which takes a spectra full of rotational transitions, finds its peaks, and determines the molecules which those peaks belong to. This was a logo I developed for the program. See Github for the full repository.

SubLIME, Sublimation of Laboratory Ices Millimeter submilimeter Experiment, is a laboratory at NASA Goddard code 691 (Astrobiology). I developed this logo while working for SubLIME, while I developed DARMA. If you go on a tour of Goddard and stop at SubLIME, you may recieved a sticker or t-shirt with my logo on it!

JWST TSS, James Webb Space Telescope Team Solar System, is a coalition of scientists within NASA Goddard that focuses on projects which advances solar system science with JWST data. I developed many promotional items for them (including: logos, t-shirts, and iron-on patches)!

Outreach

'It is the supreme art of the teacher to awaken joy in creative expression and knowledge' - Albert Einstein

Communicating science to the public is one of my most favorite activities! In this image I am giving a talk on An Introduction to Astronomy and the Night Sky at Mount Laguna Observatory. Throughout my lecture, I enaged the audience with astronomy trivia questions where they had the opportunity to win prizes (e.g. NASA posters, solar eclipse glasses, and space stickers)!

I have found a strong need to connect students with the scientific applications of skills obtained in introductory technical classes such as chemistry, calculus, or physics. For example, within this spectroscopy lab I first teach the fundamentals so that I can explain its applications to understanding the compositions of astronomical objects like exoplanet atmospheres and comets!

Astronomy is a discipline which is noted for its technical feats and rich history. Keeping up with this wide scope is incredibly important when communicating with the public at stargazing events. Throughout the night, I will answer many questions about astronomy with people of all ages with varying backgrounds in astronomy.
